Air Quality

Aim

The aim of this project is to analyze the spatial and temporal patterns of air pollution for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10 over France using CAMS reanalysis data. Data in NetCDF format are processed and visualized through QGIS by converting them into raster layers using the native mesh layer functionalities. The project culminates in the creation of average concentration maps for each pollutant, which enable the evaluation of air quality trends and the identification of critical areas in relation to current EU air quality standards.
We will use land the multi-temporal air quality data (Jan 2013 to Dec 2022) from CAMS Reanalysis Data .

Processing

Processing and Visualization of CAMS Air Quality Data Using NetCDF and QGIS

The NetCDF (Network Common Data Form) format is a widely adopted standard in atmospheric and environmental sciences for storing multidimensional scientific datasets, such as time series of pollutant concentrations across spatial grids. It is particularly suitable for handling data from services like CAMS (Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service) due to its efficiency in managing large-scale model outputs and its support for self-describing metadata (e.g., units, variable descriptions).

In the context of the project, CAMS NetCDF files were imported and processed in QGIS as mesh layers, which allow for the visualization and manipulation of time-varying geospatial data. After assigning the EPSG:4326 coordinate reference system, the dataset was explored and processed using tools such as the Mesh Calculator to compute monthly average concentrations of selected pollutants. These aggregated datasets were subsequently exported as GeoTIFF raster files, resulting in a series of average concentration maps. This approach enabled a clear and accessible spatial representation of air pollution levels.

Yearly Concentration Maps and Health Guidelines

Yearly concentration maps are created to represent the annual average levels of air pollutants categorized into standardized classes based on EU air quality guidelines. In this project, all processing was performed entirely in QGIS. After generating the yearly average rasters from monthly data, each map was reclassified into five concentration levels using the “Reclassify by Table” tool. This step allows for a clear and consistent visualization of pollution severity across space and time. The resulting maps help identify areas with critical pollution levels, support comparison across years, and provide a useful basis for environmental assessment and policy evaluation.

Annual Average Difference from 5-year Mean

It is an indicator that shows how air quality in a specific year deviates from the recent historical average over the past five years. It does this by calculating the difference between the annual average concentration of a pollutant in a given year and the mean concentration during the preceding five-years. This measure is particularly useful for highlighting short-term trends or anomalies in air quality, as it can reveal unusual changes that may warrant further investigation. For example, it can help identify the impact of extraordinary events or policies, like lockdowns or new environmental regulations, by showing whether pollution levels have improved or worsened compared to the recent past. Interpreting the results, positive values indicate that pollution levels are higher than the five-year average, suggesting possible deterioration in air quality or exceptional circumstances such as wildfires or increased traffic. Conversely, negative values indicate lower pollution compared to the average, pointing to improvements or temporary reductions often linked to factors like lockdowns or favorable weather conditions. For communicating these differences clearly on maps it has been created a diststinct visual style based on these intervals:

Overall, the analysis shows a clear improvement in NO₂ concentrations across most of France, likely reflecting the impact of reduced traffic emissions or environmental regulations. In contrast, PM 2.5 levels have increased in several regions, especially in the southeast, and PM10 concentrations show minor increases in most of the areas. These patterns highlight the importance of continuous monitoring and targeted air quality policies.
