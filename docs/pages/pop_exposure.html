Population exposure

Aim

The aim of the following analysis is to analyse the population exposure to air pollution, emphasizing areas with pollutant exceedances relative to Health guidelines over France. To better highlight the correlation we will use the bivariate map, and a corresponding pie-chart.


Health Guidelines - EEA

The European Environment Agency (EEA) has set health guidelines for air pollutants to protect human health and the environment. The EU reports their annual concentration maps according to concentration levels of NO2, PM2.5 and PM10:

Concentration levels NO2 [µg/m³] PM2.5 [µg/m³] PM10 [µg/m³]
1 [c] ≤ 10 [c] ≤ 5 [c] ≤ 15
2 10 < [c] ≤ 25 5 < [c] ≤ 10 15 < [c] ≤ 30
3 25 < [c] ≤ 40 10 < [c] ≤ 20 30 < [c] ≤ 40
4 40 < [c] ≤ 50 20 < [c] ≤ 25 40 < [c] ≤ 50
5 [c] > 50 [c] > 25 [c] > 50

Processing

Download of population data

We started by downloading the data from WorldPop.org, we were interested in the data of 2020 for the whole extent of France.

Reclassification in 5 classes

In order to obtain a bivariate map, we need to reclassify the population map into 5 broader classes.


The reclassification is done by dividing the population based on 5 quantiles.

Bivariate map data

To visualize a bivariate map we need in input both the population map and the pollutant concentration of 2020, divided in 5 classes, that we obtained in the Air Quality page.

  • See how we obtained the yearly average

Using the GAUL L2 data, obtained from FAO Gaul, meaning the data of Global Administrative Unit Layers at province level, it is possible to compute the raster statistics for the pollutants and population.
With the following formula we get the value for the bivariate map:
max of concentration classes × 10 + median of population classes

Based on the obtained value, we can assign a color to each region, as combination of the concentration and population classes
