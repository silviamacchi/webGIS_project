    Aim

    The aim of this project is to analyze the spatial and temporal patterns of air pollution for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10 over France using CAMS reanalysis data. Data in NetCDF format are processed and visualized through QGIS and the project culminates in the creation of average concentration maps for each pollutant, which enable the evaluation of air quality trends and the identification of critical areas in relation to current EU air quality standards.
    We will use land the multi-temporal air quality data (Jan 2013 to Dec 2022) from CAMS Reanalysis Data .

    Processing

    Processing and Visualization of CAMS Air Quality Data Using NetCDF and QGIS

    The work began by importing the CAMS dataset into QGIS in NetCDF format, which includes hourly pollution data for December 2022. Once loaded as a mesh layer and assigned the correct coordinate system (EPSG:4326), the data could be explored over time, showing how pollutant levels changed hour by hour in the selected area.

    To make the dataset easier to work with, the hourly values were then averaged using the Mesh Calculator. This produced a single layer showing the average pollution levels for the whole month. The result will be used for the next steps of the project and so it was exported as GeoTIFF.

    Yearly Concentration Maps and Health Guidelines

    Yearly concentration maps are generated to represent the annual average levels of air pollutants, categorized into standardized classes based on EU air quality guidelines. The European Environment Agency (EEA) has established health-based thresholds for key pollutants to protect both human health and the environment. The EU publishes annual concentration maps for major pollutants such as NO2, PM2.5, and PM10, according to these thresholds:

    Concentration levels NO₂ [µg/m³] PM2.5 [µg/m³] PM10 [µg/m³]
    1 [c] ≤ 10 [c] ≤ 5 [c] ≤ 15
    2 10 < [c] ≤ 25 5 < [c] ≤ 10 15 < [c] ≤ 30
    3 25 < [c] ≤ 40 10 < [c] ≤ 20 30 < [c] ≤ 40
    4 40 < [c] ≤ 50 20 < [c] ≤ 25 40 < [c] ≤ 50
    5 [c] > 50 [c] > 25 [c] > 50

    Annual Average Difference from 5-year Mean

    This indicator shows how air quality, in a given year, differs from the recent five-year average. It's especially useful for detecting short-term trends or anomalies, helping to identify unusual changes that may require further investigation. Positive values indicate higher pollution levels than the five-year average, possibly pointing to worsening air quality or exceptional events like wildfires or traffic surges. Negative values, on the other hand, suggest an improvement. To clearly communicate these differences on maps, a dedicated visual style has been developed based this specific value intervals:

    NO₂ [µg/m³] PM2.5 [µg/m³] PM10 [µg/m³]
    [aad] ≤ -5 [aad] ≤ -3 [aad] ≤ -10
    -5 < [aad] ≤ -2 -3 < [aad] ≤ -1.5 -10 < [aad] ≤ -4
    -2 < [aad] ≤ 0 -0.5 < [aad] ≤ 0 -4 < [aad] ≤ 0
    0 < [aad] ≤ 2 0 < [aad] ≤ 1.5 0 < [aad] ≤ 4
    2 < [aad] ≤ 5 1.5 < [aad] ≤ 3 4 < [aad] ≤ 10
    [aad] > 5 [aad] > 3 [aad] > 10

    Overall, the analysis shows a clear improvement in NO₂ concentrations across most of France, likely reflecting the impact of reduced traffic emissions or environmental regulations. In contrast, PM2.5 levels have increased in several regions, especially in the southeast, and also PM10 concentrations show an increase in most of the areas.
